<h3 id="where-did-the-word-hobby-come-from"><strong>Where did the word “Hobby” come from?</strong></h3>


<p> The origin of the word “hobby” actually dates back to the 1660s and <a href="https://www.etymonline.com/word/hobby" target="_blank" rel="noopener">involved a horse (better known as a hobbyhorse)</a>. </p>



<p>Essentially, hobby horses were created for children to mimic riding a real horse. </p>



<p>This evolved into the word “hobby” being adopted into the official language as a way to describe leisurely recreational activities.</p>



<p>In a way, people don’t select hobbies; their natural interests in life pick hobbies for them.</p>



<p>That doesn’t mean we can’t try to find a new one! Also impressive how this word has developed to include a list of hobbies and interests that are well over 1,000+! </p>

<p>To illustrate that everyone has hobbies already, or at the very least what a hobby can be for you, I bet that you can name at least ONE hobby that a friend or family member of yours has.</p>



<p><em>Don’t believe me?</em></p>



<p>Fine, let me try it out!</p>


<h3 style="text-transform:capitalize" id="hobby-examples">Hobby Examples:</h3>


<ul><li>My best friend dedicates massive amounts of time to <a aria-label="mastering the guitar (opens in a new tab)" href="https://www.boredombusted.com/hobbies/learning-guitar//" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">mastering the guitar</a>. He practices, gigs, and is constantly playing his guitars. He also dedicates a lot of his extra “fun money” in new guitar equipment. </li><li>My sister LOVES dogs. So much so that she created a profession out of her hobby by training dogs for a living! How cool is that?</li><li>My friend loves running for a hobby (no idea why!), and is really good at it! She runs because she enjoys it, but also for healthy exercise.</li><li>I have two other friends who are absolutely obsessed with all things theater. They go to all the shows in town (and sometimes out of town!), act in their own shows, listen to the music, follow the actors, and even travel to New York to go on Broadway whenever they can.</li><li>My uncle has several hobbies people can relate to. He collects and listens to vinyl records, knows everything there is about audio equipment, loves Jazz music and its history, enjoys <a href="https://www.boredombusted.com/beginner-photography-hobby/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">amateur photography</a>, and travels around the world when time permits.</li></ul>



<p>So, as you can see from this list of hobbies – a hobby can be literally anything you want it to be.</p>



<p>As long as you consider yourself someone that dedicates a fair amount of time to that subject or activity, you’ve got yourself a hobby!</p>
